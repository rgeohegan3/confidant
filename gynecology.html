Should I prepare?

Come as you are! If you shave regularly then do your thing, if you don't that works too. Whatevevr you do to maintain your female hygiene, keep doing it, no need to change any of it before you go. Instead you should prepare by reviewing your medical history (past surgeries, medicines, family history, etc.) and gathering all the concerns and questions you'd like to address. If you're having a hard time thinking of questions, check out the question 'What questions should I ask?' below.

Can I request a female physician?

Yes! When you do your research for the the doctor you choose to make your appointment with. You can choose to schedule with a female physcian. Note that your options of a provider may be based on your coverage or medical insurance plan

When should I schedule my first appointment?

There is no specific age when it comes to scheduling your first appointment. Instead, you can schedule your appointment when you need help. We have a few examples below of what needing help could look like:

  • if you have become sexualy active
  • if you are having menstrual problems (such as a lot pain, excessive bleeding, or no periods at all)
  • abnormal discharge
  • skin problems

Make sure to ask when you should schedule your next appointment.

What should I expect for a general appointment?

You provider will need to collect as much information as it takes to give you the best advice, so What your first exam will consist of will heavily depend upon the purpose of your visit. Some exams may include a pelvic exam and a breast examination, and others may only be a conversation.

Will it hurt?

Let your provider know if you are concerned about pain. Your first visit may or may not include a speculum exam depending on the nature of your visit. The speculum is the about the width of a penis or vibrator and is inserted so the doctor can get a better look at the inside of the vagina. If you've already engaged in sexual activity, there should be no pain associated with the speculum exam. If you haven't and/or are still concerned about the pain, your doctor may use a pediatric speculum, a physical exam to feel the internal organs with gloved fingers, or a sonogram depending upon the situation.

What questions should I ask?

Having trouble thinking of questions to ask on your first gynecology visit? Here are some samples:

  • Ask for general health advice
  • Are all my parts formed properly?
  • How do I avoid STIs?
  • How do I address the menstrual problems I'm experiencing?
  • Can I get tested for STIs?
  • How can I have healthy sexual activity?
  • How can I avoid pregnancy?
  • What immunizations do I need? (Like the HPV vaccine which can help prevent cervical cancers)
  • What type of testing should I be doing to screen for any future problems? (Like a pap smear to test for cervical cancer)
